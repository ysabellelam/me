Student Orgs

In an effort to help students at UCSD, I have served on the boards of multiple engineering orgs. Currently, I have helped start ACM(one of the world's largest computng organizations) at UCSD, where I serve as the financial as well as the communications director. I also serve as the Triton Engineering Student Council's Council Lead, where I work with leaders of other engineering orgs to help solve issues in Jacob's School of Engineering.

Previously, I was elected as the R&D Director for Project In a Box, where I managed around 10 project teams, doing research and protoyping in computer, electrical, and mechanical engineering. In addition, I was the public relations chair for UCSD's IEEE Student Branch, where I was later voted officer of the year.

STEM Education

Outside of UCSD, I work with K-12 students to teach them engineering skills. I have worked with both Treobytes and Hands on Tech to teach kids in both a camp based and classroom based setting, to teach kids the FUNdamentals of different fields in engineering. Topics include: CAD, 3D printing, Robotics, Microcontollers, Artificial Intelligence, Virtual Reality, Computer programming (HTML, CSS, Python, C) Computer hardware, IT fundamnetals, and Entrupenuership. Beyond teaching, I also develop parts of the ciriculum that are used to teach the students.
